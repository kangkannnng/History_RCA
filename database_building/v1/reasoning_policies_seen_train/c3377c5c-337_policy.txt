[Trigger]
- Log anomaly pattern: Repeated "context deadline exceeded" errors in frontend service logs when attempting to retrieve ads, with messages indicating RPC timeouts.
- Metric anomaly pattern: Sharp increase in response time metrics (rrt and rrt_max) for adservice, with values spiking up to 90 times normal, and a significant drop in request and response counts.
- Trace anomaly pattern: Traces reveal adservice calls experiencing extreme latency, increasing from an average of 5.5 seconds to over 500 seconds, accompanied by numerous timeout status codes.

[Focus Evidence]
Primary evidence (core causal indicators):
- Evidence 1: Trace data shows adservice call latency increased by 90 times, with over 1300 "context deadline exceeded" errors – this directly indicates severe service degradation and unresponsiveness.
- Evidence 2: Logs consistently report RPC timeouts when frontend services call adservice, with error messages like "failed to get ads," pointing to adservice being unable to respond within deadlines.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: Other services such as PaymentService and CartService show increased latency – these are likely downstream effects or collateral damage due to adservice issues, as their delays are less severe and correlated with adservice failures.
- Evidence 4: Node memory metrics indicate resource constraints on some nodes, but the adservice degradation is disproportionately extreme and specific, suggesting it is not primarily caused by general resource scarcity.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the massive latency spike and numerous timeout errors in adservice calls, we suspect a critical failure in the adservice component causing it to be unresponsive."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by general network issues, we would expect uniform latency increases across all service calls; however, actual observation shows adservice calls are disproportionately affected with 90x latency increase, while other services show minor delays. This rules out widespread network problems as the root cause."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of trace data showing adservice latency explosion and logs confirming timeout errors indicates that adservice experienced a severe performance breakdown, leading to cascading failures in dependent services."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is a severe degradation in adservice performance, making it unable to respond within expected timeframes, which propagated errors to frontend services and caused broader system impact."
