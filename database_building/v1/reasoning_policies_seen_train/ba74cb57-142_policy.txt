[Focus Evidence]
Primary evidence (core causal indicators):
- Evidence 1: pod_processes metric change from 1.0 to 2.0 for a service instance - this matters because it provides a direct indicator of a restart event, which can lead to temporary service unavailability.
- Evidence 2: Trace data showing connection refused errors specifically when checkoutservice calls productcatalogservice - this matters as it directly demonstrates service-level connectivity failures linked to the restarting instance.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: Increased error ratios and response times in other services such as adservice and checkoutservice - this is a consequence of the root cause service being unavailable, as dependent services experience failures.
- Evidence 4: Memory availability metrics showing fluctuations in node resources - this can be deprioritized as while it may correlate, it does not directly explain the abrupt connection errors and restart event.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the observed increase in pod_processes metric and connection refused errors in traces, we suspect that a service instance restart is causing temporary unavailability, leading to failures in dependent services."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by general network congestion, we would expect widespread latency or errors across multiple service interactions; however, the errors are concentrated around calls to productcatalogservice, ruling out broad network issues."
"If the issue stemmed from gradual resource exhaustion, we would anticipate progressive performance degradation; but the abrupt nature of connection refused errors aligns with the timing of a restart event indicated by the metric change."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of the metric-based restart indicator and the trace-based connection errors indicates that a service instance became unavailable due to a restart, which cascaded into failures for services relying on it."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is a service instance restart that resulted in temporary unavailability, causing connection refused errors and subsequent failures in dependent services during critical operations."
