[Trigger]
- Log anomaly pattern: Frontend services repeatedly log "context deadline exceeded" errors when calling the adservice, accompanied by adservice logs showing "Agent failed to start!" and "java.lang.reflect.InvocationTargetException" entries.
- Metric anomaly pattern: Response time metrics (rrt and rrt_max) for frontend services show dramatic increases, with adservice-related latency metrics spiking from normal levels to extremely high values.
- Trace anomaly pattern: Distributed traces reveal that calls from frontend to adservice experience latency increases of over 90 times the normal duration, with thousands of timeout errors recorded.

[Focus Evidence]
Primary evidence (core causal indicators):
- Evidence 1: Trace data indicates adservice call latency surged from approximately 5.5 seconds to around 500 seconds, with 2359 instances of "context deadline exceeded" errors – this shows severe degradation in adservice responsiveness, directly pointing to its inability to process requests.
- Evidence 2: Adservice logs contain errors such as "Agent failed to start!" and exceptions like "InvocationTargetException", suggesting internal runtime failures during service initialization or operation, which align with the observed unresponsiveness.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: Frontend services exhibit elevated response times and error rates – these are consequences of adservice failures, as frontend depends on adservice, confirming the propagation of issues upstream.
- Evidence 4: Minor latency increases in other services like paymentservice – these are likely secondary effects due to system-wide load or cascading failures, not primary causes, and can be deprioritized.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the extreme latency spikes and timeout errors specific to adservice calls, along with internal adservice log errors, we suspect that the adservice is experiencing critical internal execution failures leading to service unavailability."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by network connectivity issues between services, we would expect to see widespread timeouts across multiple service dependencies or irregular error patterns; however, the errors are concentrated on adservice with consistent internal log indicators, ruling out network-related causes."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of adservice's internal runtime errors and the direct evidence of massive latency increases and timeouts in service calls indicates that the root cause is internal to the adservice, involving execution delays or failures that prevent it from responding to requests."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is internal execution failure within the adservice, resulting in complete unresponsiveness and causing timeout errors in dependent frontend services due to the inability to process requests."
