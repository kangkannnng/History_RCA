- Evidence 1: Trace data shows a specific write operation to a storage component experienced a 20x latency increase - this points directly to a severe performance degradation in a foundational service.
- Evidence 2: The absence of error logs coinciding with the metric spikes indicates the issue is related to resource or performance constraints, not functional failures.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: The cartservice error ratios spiked dramatically - this is a consequence of its dependency on the degraded storage component, not an independent failure.
- Evidence 4: Other services like currencyservice and shippingservice showed increased response times - these are likely secondary effects propagated through the call chain, not primary causes.

[Reasoning]

"Given the extreme latency increase for a critical write operation to a shared storage component, we suspect a severe performance bottleneck in that underlying data layer."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by a code defect in the cartservice itself, we would expect to see error logs or failures specific to its logic; However, actual observation shows no error logs and the issue manifests as latency in storage operations; This rules out a primary application logic failure in the dependent service."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of the storage operation's severe latency degradation and the consequent spike in error rates for its exclusive consumer indicates that a performance bottleneck in the foundational storage layer propagated upward, causing cascading failures in dependent services."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is a severe performance degradation in a critical storage component, which created a bottleneck that cascaded upward to cause timeouts and errors in services that depend exclusively on it for data operations."
