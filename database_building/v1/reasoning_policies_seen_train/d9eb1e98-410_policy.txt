"Given the extreme latency increase in data storage operations and the concurrent error rate spike in a dependent service, we suspect a fundamental degradation in data storage performance affecting critical workflows."

"If this were caused by application-level issues or resource contention on nodes, we would expect to see corresponding error logs or uniform resource metric anomalies across services; however, no critical error logs were detected, and resource metrics did not consistently indicate saturation. This rules out isolated application failures or broad resource exhaustion as the root cause."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of disproportionate latency in storage operations and the direct impact on service error rates indicates that the root cause is a severe performance degradation in the underlying data storage layer, which propagated to dependent services through delayed operations."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is a critical slowdown in data storage operations, leading to cascading performance failures in services that rely on timely data access."
