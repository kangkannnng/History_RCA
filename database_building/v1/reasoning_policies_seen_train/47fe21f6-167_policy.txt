- Evidence 1: Log entries from adservice show explicit references to error injection and exception handling during critical operations, with high occurrence counts. This matters for causality because it directly points to internal service disruptions initiated by an external source.
- Evidence 2: Metric data for adservice reveals a sharp rise in error rates, correlating with the timing of log anomalies. This matters for causality as it quantifies the impact and confirms the service is failing consistently.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: Frontend logs contain numerous entries about failed RPC calls to adservice, with error descriptions indicating unknown issues. This is a consequence not cause because it reflects the propagation of adservice failures to dependent components.
- Evidence 4: Other metric anomalies, such as increased memory usage on certain nodes, can be deprioritized as they do not align directly with the specific error patterns in adservice and may be incidental or secondary effects.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the log patterns showing injected errors and exceptions within adservice, combined with the metric spike in error ratios, we suspect that adservice is experiencing deliberate interference causing operational failures."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by broader infrastructure issues like resource exhaustion, we would expect uniform performance degradation across multiple services on affected nodes; however, the observations show isolated error patterns centered on adservice, with other services exhibiting different anomaly types. This rules out systemic resource constraints as the primary driver."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of adservice's internal error logs and the corroborating metric increases, alongside the dependent service failures, indicates that the root cause is an external agent introducing faults into adservice, leading to its inability to process requests and cascading effects."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is an external component interfering with adservice's normal execution, resulting in repeated exceptions and service unavailability that propagates to clients."
