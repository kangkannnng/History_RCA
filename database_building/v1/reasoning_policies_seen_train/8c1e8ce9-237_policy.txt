[Trigger]
- Log anomaly pattern: adservice-0 logs show repeated execution of a rule named "AdService--stress-1749715369" and associated thread creation, alongside errors indicating an agent failed to start with an InvocationTargetException. A separate frontend log shows a deadline exceeded error when attempting to retrieve ads.
- Metric anomaly pattern: The pod_cpu_usage for adservice-0 shows a drastic increase of 193.44%, coinciding with significant degradation in its response time metrics (rrt and rrt_max increased by 146.87% and 130.3% respectively).
- Trace anomaly pattern: Calls from frontend to the AdService/GetAds endpoint exhibit a latency increase of approximately 9.25 times the normal duration during the fault period.

[Focus Evidence]
Primary evidence (core causal indicators):
- Evidence 1: Logs from adservice-0 contain multiple entries for "Rule.execute called for AdService--stress-1749715369" and related stress thread activity - This directly indicates an active process designed to induce computational load was injected and executing on this specific pod.
- Evidence 2: The pod_cpu_usage metric for adservice-0 shows an extreme spike of 193.44% - This quantifies a severe consumption of compute resources on the pod, correlating temporally with the log evidence of stress activity and degrading its capacity to process requests.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: Trace data shows frontend calls to AdService/GetAds experienced high latency, and frontend logs contain RPC deadline exceeded errors - This is a consequence of the primary degradation in adservice-0, as downstream services timed out waiting for its overloaded responses.
- Evidence 4: Other services like cartservice and redis show elevated latency in traces - These are likely secondary effects or noise within the propagation chain, as the most severe and direct resource anomaly is isolated to adservice-0.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the direct log entries referencing a 'stress' rule execution and the correlated extreme spike in CPU consumption on adservice-0, we suspect an external agent or process has injected a workload that is monopolizing the pod's computational resources."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by a generalized node-level resource shortage, we would expect to see elevated node_cpu_usage_rate or node_memory_MemAvailable_bytes anomalies affecting multiple pods on the same node. However, the node hosting adservice-0 does not show the most severe node-level metric anomalies, and the pod_cpu_usage spike is isolated and extreme. This rules out a primary node-level resource contention as the root cause and points to a process internal to the pod."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of the explicit 'stress' activity logs originating from within the adservice-0 pod and the quantified severe resource exhaustion on that same pod indicates that the root cause was an externally induced, pod-local computational overload. The subsequent service latency and timeout errors are consistent downstream effects of this primary condition."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is the injection and execution of a stress-inducing workload on adservice-0, which consumed excessive computational resources, severely degrading the pod's processing capability and causing timeouts for dependent services."
