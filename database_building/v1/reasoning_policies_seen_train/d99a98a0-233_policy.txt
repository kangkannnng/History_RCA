[Trigger]
- Log anomaly pattern: No error logs were recorded during the fault period, suggesting a silent or brief failure event.
- Metric anomaly pattern: The process count metric for a backend pod dropped from a stable value to zero, and multiple pods exhibited CPU usage and network packet transmission falling to zero.
- Trace anomaly pattern: A single service call showed improved response time, while expected calls to a dependent service were entirely absent in the trace data.

[Focus Evidence]
Primary evidence (core causal indicators):
- Evidence 1: Process count metric for a critical backend pod plummeted to zero – this directly indicates complete process interruption, which immediately halts service availability and is a primary causal signal.
- Evidence 2: Trace data revealed missing calls from a service to its dependency – this shows that the dependent pod was unreachable or unresponsive, aligning with the process termination and confirming service disruption.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: CPU usage and network activity for several frontend pods dropped to zero – this is a downstream effect as these services rely on the failed backend, leading to reduced activity, not the cause.
- Evidence 4: Improved response time in one trace call – this can be deprioritized as it likely results from reduced system load due to the failure, serving as an indirect consequence.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the process count dropping to zero for a backend pod, we suspect a sudden and complete process interruption that renders the pod incapable of serving requests."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by issues in frontend services, such as overload or misconfiguration, we would expect increased error rates or delays in traces; however, actual observations show no error logs, missing dependency calls, and even improved response times in some cases. This rules out frontend-originated problems as the primary cause."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of direct evidence from process termination and the absence of expected service calls indicates that the root cause is the abrupt cessation of processes in a key backend pod, triggering a cascade of inactivity in dependent services."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is the unexpected termination of processes in an essential service pod, which prevented it from handling requests and led to a failure cascade across dependent components."
