- Evidence 2: Metrics show that all shippingservice pods have dramatically increased error ratios, confirming that the service is experiencing failures from the client perspective - this matters because it correlates service-level errors with the connectivity issues.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the consistent connection refused errors in logs when multiple services attempt to reach shippingservice, we suspect a configuration issue that prevents successful connections to that service."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by a general resource exhaustion or performance degradation, we would expect gradual latency increases or timeouts across various services; however, actual observation shows immediate and specific connection refused errors targeting one service endpoint. This rules out resource constraints as the primary cause and points to an accessibility problem."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of logs indicating persistent connection failures to a specific endpoint and metrics confirming high error ratios for that service indicates that the service is unreachable due to incorrect endpoint configuration, leading to cascading failures in dependent workflows."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is an incorrect configuration that renders the shippingservice endpoint unreachable, causing connection refused errors and propagating failures to upstream services during critical operations."
