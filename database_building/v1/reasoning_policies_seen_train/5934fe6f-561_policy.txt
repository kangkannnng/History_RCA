[Trigger]
- Log anomaly pattern: Increased frequency of HTTP 404 error responses from frontend service instances, with hundreds of occurrences recorded.
- Metric anomaly pattern: Significant elevation in error ratios and client error ratios across all frontend pods, with P99 error rates rising from 0% to over 30% in some cases.
- Trace anomaly pattern: Notable latency increases in operations between CartService and Redis, such as HGET and HMSET calls, with delays multiplying by factors of 5 to 7 times normal levels.

[Focus Evidence]
Primary evidence (core causal indicators):
- Evidence 1: CartService to Redis operation latencies spiked dramatically, e.g., HGET from 1739ms to 8892ms – this indicates a severe performance degradation at the data storage interface, which is a critical dependency for service functionality.
- Evidence 2: Frontend services exhibited high HTTP 404 error rates, e.g., frontend-2 with 1151 errors – this suggests that client requests are being mishandled or routed incorrectly, leading to widespread failures.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: Other services like CurrencyService and ProductCatalogService showed moderate latency increases – these are likely cascading effects from the primary disruptions, as they depend on the same resource or error propagation paths.
- Evidence 4: No traditional error logs were detected during the fault period – this can be deprioritized as it may indicate that the failure mode involves timeouts or silent resource exhaustion rather than logged errors.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the concurrent spikes in frontend error responses and latency at the CartService-Redis interface, we suspect that improper request handling at the frontend is generating excessive or malformed traffic, straining downstream resources."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by a general performance issue in Redis alone, we would expect latency increases to be isolated to Redis-dependent calls without corresponding frontend errors; however, the observed pattern includes both frontend errors and cascading latencies, ruling out a standalone storage problem."
"If the frontend errors were due to intermittent client-side issues, we might see sporadic errors without systemic latency spikes; but the consistent latency degradation in CartService-Redis interactions indicates a sustained resource contention triggered by frontend behavior."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of frontend error surges and downstream latency escalations points to an initial disruption in client request processing that overloads the data layer, creating a bottleneck that propagates failures back to the frontend."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is a failure in client request validation at the frontend service, leading to erroneous request patterns that exhaust resources in the dependent data storage, resulting in latency buildups and cascading service errors."
