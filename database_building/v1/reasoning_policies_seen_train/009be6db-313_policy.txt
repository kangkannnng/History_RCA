[Trigger]
- Log anomaly pattern: Multiple error logs from cartservice instances reporting "FailedPrecondition: Can't access cart storage", and frontend services logging rpc errors with similar messages, indicating a failure in data access operations.
- Metric anomaly pattern: server_error_ratio for the storage service spiked from 0.0 to 2.03, accompanied by a drop in pod_cpu_usage from 0.13 to 0.0, suggesting service degradation or failure in the storage component.
- Trace anomaly pattern: Severe latency increases in storage operations, with HMSET delays rising 35x and HGET delays 5x, leading to cascading delays in cartservice operations (e.g., GetCart latency increased 786x) and eventual frontend HTTP 500 errors.

[Focus Evidence]
Primary evidence (core causal indicators):
- Evidence 1: Storage service metric showing server_error_ratio spike and pod_cpu_usage drop - this indicates the storage component itself is experiencing errors and potential process issues, serving as a direct indicator of source degradation.
- Evidence 2: Cartservice logs with "Can't access cart storage" errors - this directly correlates storage access failures with the metric anomalies, pointing to a dependency failure.

Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: Frontend logs showing rpc errors due to cartservice failures - these are consequences of the storage issue propagating through the service chain, not the origin.
- Evidence 4: Increased latency in checkoutservice and other intermediate services - these are downstream effects resulting from cartservice delays, helping trace the impact flow but not the cause.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the cartservice logs indicating inability to access storage and the storage service's metric spike in error ratio, we suspect a performance or availability issue in the storage layer that is preventing data access."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by a problem in the frontend services, we would expect errors isolated to frontend metrics and logs without underlying storage access failures; however, observations show storage-level errors and latency, ruling out frontend as the root cause."
"If the issue originated in cartservice independently, we would see cartservice-specific errors unrelated to storage, but the logs explicitly reference storage access failures, and trace data shows storage operation delays preceding cartservice delays."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of the storage service's error ratio spike and the direct log evidence of storage access failures, supported by trace data showing latency originating from storage operations, indicates that the root cause is a degradation in the storage component's performance or availability, which cascaded to dependent services."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is a degradation in the storage component's performance or availability, evidenced by its increased error rates and resource changes, which led to data access failures in cartservice and subsequent service disruptions upstream."
