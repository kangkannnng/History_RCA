Secondary evidence (downstream effects or noise):
- Evidence 3: Adservice metrics show increased error ratios and response times – these could be secondary effects due to system-wide stress or cascading failures from the paymentservice issue, as they lack direct correlation to the core connection errors.
- Evidence 4: Other metric changes like tidb-tikv io_util fluctuations are minor and not directly correlated with the core error pattern, so they can be deprioritized as likely unrelated noise.

[Reasoning]
Step 1: Initial hypothesis based on primary evidence
"Given the consistent 'connection refused' errors from all frontend instances targeting paymentservice, we suspect that paymentservice has a configuration or availability problem preventing it from accepting incoming connections."

Step 2: Verification or elimination
"If this were caused by a frontend service issue, we would see errors localized to specific frontend instances or different error messages; however, all frontend instances report identical connection failures to the same paymentservice endpoint. This rules out frontend as the root cause. Similarly, if adservice were the primary issue, the error patterns would not consistently point to paymentservice, and the direct connection errors in logs provide stronger evidence against it."

Step 3: Convergence to conclusion
"The combination of direct connection refusal evidence from logs and the correlated spike in frontend error metrics indicates that paymentservice is unreachable due to a service configuration error or port accessibility issue, with downstream effects explaining other metric anomalies."

[Conclusion]
"The root cause is that paymentservice is not accessible on the configured port, likely due to an incorrect service definition or network configuration change that prevents successful connections."
